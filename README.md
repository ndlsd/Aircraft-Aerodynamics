This project explores basic aerodynmic forces acting on an aircraft using MATLAB. 

In straight-and-level, four main forces act on an aircraft : Thrust, Weight, Drag and Lift. 
They are defined as follows : 
  * **Lift** : This force is produced by the dynamic interaction between the air and the airfoil (wing). This force is perpendicular (or normal) to the airflow direction. 
  * **Weight** : The force caused by gravity that pulls the aircraft toward the center of the earth. It acts vertically downward.
  * **Drag** : The aerodynamic resistance created by the aircraft as it moves through the air. It is caused by the disruption of airflow around the aircraft components (wings, fuselage...). Drag acts in the direction opposite to the aircraft's motion.
  * **Thrust** : The force generated by the aircraft engines that propels the aircraft forward and allows it to overcome drag.

To resume, Lift and Weight act in the vertical direction and determine whether the aircraft can stay in the air, while Thrust and Drag act in the horizontal direction and determine the aircraft's speed.
